## Reflection

### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by gathering stakeholder requirements, identifying the problems users are trying to solve, and translating those needs into software functionality. User stories help bridge the gap between customer requirements and development work by describing requirements from the user's perspective. During the SNHU Travel project, customer requests for destination recommendations, travel preferences, and pricing options were transformed into user stories that guided development priorities. User stories helped ensure that development remained focused on delivering value to users rather than simply implementing technical features.

### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach software development by first understanding project requirements and then breaking larger objectives into smaller, manageable tasks. One of the most valuable lessons from this course was the importance of adaptability. During the SNHU Travel project, management shifted the project's focus toward wellness and detox travel after development had already begun. Scrum-Agile practices allowed the team to adapt to changing priorities without abandoning completed work. In future projects, I hope to incorporate Agile practices such as backlog management, sprint planning, stakeholder feedback, and retrospectives because they encourage continuous improvement while helping development remain aligned with user and business needs.

### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating effectively, collaborating with others, and contributing toward shared project goals. Successful software development depends on more than technical skills alone. Team members must be willing to share information, ask questions when requirements are unclear, and support one another in solving problems. During the SNHU Travel project, communication between the Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developer, and Tester helped clarify requirements, address changing priorities, and reduce misunderstandings. A good team member contributes their own expertise while remaining open to feedback and committed to helping the team succeed.
